Seasons are defined by seasonality by the index frequency. The tsibble is 1M with an implicit annual cycle. So each season is one calendar month and the cycle length is 12. This is mechanical rather than physical. The prompt feasts doesn’t know may-june is typically when snow melts it just knows that month 5 and month 6 are the 5th and 6th place of 12. For the seasonal shape there is a unimodal annual cycle which peaks from May-June with the rising limb starting in April and receding in July back to base flow in October. The timing of the peak is relatively stable from year to year but the amplitude varies a lot.There is two parts to the physical processes of the May-June peak. First, snowmelt is the main driver from the surrounding mountains. The secondary process is the hydrograph is shaped by operations which are timed to agricultural needs and demands
